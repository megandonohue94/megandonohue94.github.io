Senior Design Project

My Senior Design Project was completed over 9 months in a group of 5 students during my senior year at Drexel University. The design looked to promote spinal cord repair through directional guidance using suture silk. Spinal cord injury (SCI) affects 347,000 people in the U.S.A. There is minimal regenerative functional recovery due to the non-permissive tissue enviornmnet after a SCI.
A creo model and a mathematical packing model were created to model the design and presence of channels in the scaffold. Three design solutions were created: heat shrink wrap tubing (pictured left), spiral bound, and braid. Verification testing included axon testing with Dorsal Root Ganglion (DRG), mechanical testing to analyze stiffness, channel size testing using SEM, and swelling testing to ensure the channels were not being occluded.
